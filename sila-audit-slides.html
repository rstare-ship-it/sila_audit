Cross-Portfolio Patterns

Issues Appearing Across the Most Tenants

Three issues are universal — present in all 7 tenants: Sold Estimates not booked, Sales Margin Control not active, and outdated Integrations. These represent the highest-priority opportunities for portfolio-wide impact.

Focus Area 01

Revenue Leakage is a Portfolio-Wide Problem

Across all 7 tenants, significant revenue is being left on the table through missed bookings, untracked estimates, and lapsed membership programs.

  • Sold Estimates Not Booked — 7/7 tenants. Over $12M in sold work from last year never converted to jobs. Ranges from $46k to $5M per tenant.
  • Unsold Estimate Follow-Up — 6/7 tenants. Millions in the 30-day pipeline with no recorded follow-up in ServiceTitan.
  • Membership Visits Backlog — 5/7 tenants. ~23,000 visits not fulfilled last year, eroding membership value and renewal rates.
  • Expired Memberships Not Followed Up — 4/7 tenants. Thousands of lapsed customers with no re-engagement activity in ST.

Universal · 7/7 Tenants

Activate Sales Margin Control

No tenant in the portfolio has Sales Margin Control active. Without it, discounts and pricing overrides go unchecked, quietly eroding margins on every job.

  • Single-fix setup — low effort, one-time configuration
  • Prevents accidental under-pricing by technicians
  • Sets floor margins by business unit or pricebook category
  • Immediate protection with no workflow disruption
Affects: All 7 tenants · Owner: General Manager / Owner

Focus Area 04 — Inventory & Purchasing

Inventory Workflows Are Underused Across the Portfolio

Inventory and purchasing features exist in ServiceTitan to drive cost control and field efficiency — but they're widely underutilized or misconfigured across Sila tenants.

  • Purchase Orders Outstanding — 3/7 tenants have hundreds to thousands of pending POs, some going back to 2021. T-Mark has 6,000+ outstanding POs.
  • Replenishment Module Not Used — Trucks aren't being replenished through ServiceTitan at several tenants, causing parts to be added after the fact or missed entirely.
  • Items Not in Pricebook Categories — Items without categories are invisible to technicians in the field. This contributes directly to missed materials charges and inaccurate job costing.
  • Inventory Counts Not Performed — Multiple tenants have Inventory turned on but no counts being run, making inventory balances unreliable for cost reporting.
  • Default Replenishment Vendor — 2 tenants have materials assigned to a generic "Default" vendor instead of actual vendors, breaking PO and cost workflows.
