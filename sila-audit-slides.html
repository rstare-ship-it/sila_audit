Audit Scope & Methodology

Advisory Services Audit — What We Did

ServiceTitan's Advisory Services team conducted a structured deep-dive review of all 7 Sila Brands tenant instances, analyzing platform configuration, workflow hygiene, and financial operations against best practices.

  • Platform Configuration Review — Compared each tenant's feature settings, integrations, and security posture against ServiceTitan best practices.
  • Workflow Hygiene Analysis — Identified open backlogs, unprocessed queues, and breakdown points in the job lifecycle from booking through invoicing.
  • Financial Workflow Audit — Reviewed AR invoices, export status, GL account mapping, returns, and billing run configurations.
  • Cross-Tenant Pattern Matching — Catalogued each finding by category and cross-referenced across all 7 tenants to identify portfolio-wide patterns vs. isolated issues.
  • Effort vs. Impact Scoring — Each finding was scored by effort required and revenue / efficiency effect to prioritize the action plan.

Focus Area 02 — Dispatch

Why Job Status Hygiene Matters

📋 Job Status
  • No job left behind means no revenue left behind — every open status is a potential uncollected dollar.
  • Watch for patterns by business unit — high on-hold rates in one BU often signal a training or accountability gap.
  • Establish daily, weekly & monthly review cadences with ownership at each service center.
  • Job tags and task management in ST ensure visibility and accountability across the whole team.
⏸ On Hold
  • Putting jobs on Hold makes sense for permits or parts — but they should never stay on Hold beyond 30 days without review.
  • Keep on-hold jobs updated with Notes, Tags, and Task Management so nothing gets forgotten.
  • Jobs on hold for 60+ days with no update should be evaluated for cancellation or closure.
  • Cancel or close jobs that will never be run — they pollute reporting and dispatch views.
🔄 In Progress
  • There should be no In Progress jobs from 2024 or prior — these represent revenue that was earned but never finalized.
  • Old in-progress jobs are typically caused by additional appointments added after completion, or incomplete technician timesheets.
  • Each in-progress job from a prior year requires individual review to confirm completion and close correctly.
  • Closing these unlocks accurate revenue reporting for those periods.
📅 Scheduled Past
  • Jobs scheduled in the past mean dispatch board pollution — CSRs and dispatchers can't get a clean view of today's real workload.
  • Use Business Unit and Tag filters to surface past-dated jobs and ensure they are rescheduled or cancelled.
  • Sila tenants: 5 of 7 have jobs on the schedule with dates that have already passed.
  • Cancel those that will not be run — rescheduling stale jobs keeps the board actionable.

How Audit Gaps Connect

How These Issues Corrupt Job Costing

🗂️
Reporting Impact

GL Inconsistencies → Unreliable Job Margins

When pricebook items are mapped to wrong GL accounts, the revenue and COGS sides of a job don't tell a coherent story. Job margin reports can't be trusted for pricing or compensation decisions.

  • Equipment billed as "Parts" understates equipment revenue line
  • COGS categories mixed up → P&L breaks at the BU level
  • Management decisions made on incorrect margin data
📦
Cost Attribution Impact

Unreceived POs → Missing Material Costs

When purchase orders are not received in ServiceTitan, the cost of materials never flows to the job. The job appears more profitable than it actually is — creating phantom margins.

  • T-Mark: 6,000+ outstanding POs = massive cost attribution gap
  • Completed jobs show no material cost → misleading tech performance data
  • AP reconciliation becomes nearly impossible
🏷️
Field Execution Impact

Pricebook Items Without Categories → Missed Charges

Items not assigned to pricebook categories are invisible to technicians in the field. When techs can't find items, they either skip the charge or add informal notes — both result in missing job cost data.

  • 5/7 tenants have items outside proper category structure
  • Missed material charges reduce actual revenue collected per job
  • Replenishment can't run correctly without proper categorization
📊
What Accurate Job Costing Enables

Why Fixing This Matters

Clean job costing is the foundation of every business decision in ServiceTitan. When it's working correctly, leadership can see what's actually happening.

  • Accurate profit/loss per job, tech, and business unit
  • Pricing decisions backed by real margin data
  • Reliable P&L feeds your accounting system correctly
  • Identifies high vs. low margin job types for growth focus

Focus Area 05 — Inventory & Purchasing

Inventory Workflows Are Underused Across the Portfolio

Inventory and purchasing features exist in ServiceTitan to drive cost control and field efficiency — but they're widely underutilized or misconfigured across Sila tenants.

  • Purchase Orders Outstanding — 3/7 tenants have hundreds to thousands of pending POs, some going back to 2021. T-Mark has 6,000+ outstanding POs.
  • Replenishment Module Not Used — Trucks aren't being replenished through ServiceTitan at several tenants, causing parts to be added after the fact or missed entirely.
  • Items Not in Pricebook Categories — Items without categories are invisible to technicians in the field. This contributes directly to missed materials charges and inaccurate job costing.
  • Inventory Counts Not Performed — Multiple tenants have Inventory turned on but no counts being run, making inventory balances unreliable for cost reporting.
  • Default Replenishment Vendor — 2 tenants have materials assigned to a generic "Default" vendor instead of actual vendors, breaking PO and cost workflows.
