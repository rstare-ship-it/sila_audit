Context: In ServiceTitan, a Purchase Order moves from Pending → Exported/Sent (sent to vendor) → Received. POs sitting in "Exported" or "Sent" status have been sent out but never marked as received in the system. This can mean (1) the goods were physically received but the PO was never closed in ServiceTitan, (2) the vendor hasn't shipped, or (3) the order was partially or incorrectly fulfilled. Either way, these represent unresolved open commitments that affect inventory accuracy and cost tracking.

Key Observations by Vendor
F.W. Webb — $2M / 6,542 POs
The largest single vendor with the most POs. Many are Supply House Run / Deliver types. The volume suggests a systemic gap in receiving workflow — technicians picking up parts at the counter but dispatchers not closing POs. Recommend a weekly reconciliation with Webb account rep.
Lennox Industries — $1.37M / 802 POs
Lennox is the primary HVAC equipment supplier. At $1.37M unreceived, these are likely high-value equipment units (furnaces, air handlers) that were installed but POs were never marked received. This directly ties to the negative margin jobs in HVAC Install. Each unreceived Lennox PO = incorrect job cost reporting.
KINETICO — $546K / 167 POs
KINETICO water treatment units are sold on a consignment/special-order basis. Unreceived POs here likely mean units were delivered to customer locations but receipts weren't posted back. Reconcile against Water Installation jobs completed.
T&J Construction — $305K / 76 POs
A subcontractor/construction vendor with high average PO value ($4,023). Unresolved subcontractor POs create AP liability exposure. These should be confirmed against work completion and invoices.
Citizens Bank Credit Card — 335 POs
Credit card purchases tracked as POs suggest miscellaneous field purchases. These are hard to control and reconcile. May warrant a policy review on credit card PO processes.
