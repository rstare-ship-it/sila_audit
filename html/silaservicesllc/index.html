Key Findings & Actions
🚨 Critical — Estimates Follow-Up
54.3% of 110,938 open estimates have zero follow-up contacts recorded — $445.9M in pipeline that has never been re-contacted since the initial estimate. The Sales-HVAC BU alone holds $235.7M across a large open estimate pool. A 2% close rate on estimates under 1 year would add $4.5M+ incremental revenue without additional marketing spend.
⚠️ Attention — Outstanding Balance
$543,843 outstanding across 116 completed jobs. Completed jobs with unpaid balances represent work delivered but not fully collected. A systematic AR follow-up on these 116 accounts is needed — most are likely invoiced and contactable, making this a high-probability recovery.
⚠️ Attention — Membership ARR Mix
Of 10,305 active members, 6,123 (59.4%) are One Time billing — warranty and install products with no recurring revenue. True recurring base is 4,182 members. The 38,988 expired and canceled membership pool represents a large re-engagement opportunity, particularly for cross-sell to recurring maintenance plans.
✅ Strength — Gross Margin
70.7% gross margin across $82.7M in completed job revenue is the strongest in the Sila portfolio. HVAC Install RREP drives $58.7M at 68.6% GM. The HVAC Service BU delivers 80.2% GM and Plumbing 82.1% GM — indicating strong pricing discipline and efficient field operations throughout the organization.
✅ Strength — Job Queue Health
Zero stale open jobs detected — all 2,352 current open jobs (50 Hold, 2,259 Scheduled, 43 In Progress) are active and current. This is the cleanest open job queue in the entire Sila portfolio and indicates strong dispatch hygiene, scheduling discipline, and job closure practices.
⚠️ Attention — Inventory POs
$1.17M in unreceived POs with ~99% over 180 days old — consistent with the portfolio-wide pattern of goods received in-field but not marked received in ServiceTitan. While the lowest absolute exposure in the portfolio, the same workflow gap exists. Ferguson HVAC ($420K) and Lennox ($193K) are the top vendors to audit.
